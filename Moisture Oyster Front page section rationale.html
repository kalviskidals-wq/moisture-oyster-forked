Page Structure

A 9-block conversion-first homepage for the US DTC market. Every block serves a specific role in the journey from first impression to purchase intent — and is built to scale as the product catalog grows.

01Navigation

The permanent brand frame across every page. Category labels (Eye Drops, Vitamins) are introduced from launch even with one product per category — creating the navigation architecture that grows into a full dropdown at Phase 2 without structural disruption. Bag and CTA anchor the right side.

02Hero Section

The most critical 5–7 seconds of the brand experience. A split layout gives equal real estate to the product (left: headline, value proposition, CTA) and the brand lifestyle (right: product imagery top, lifestyle tone bottom). A single primary CTA reduces decision fatigue and directs traffic immediately into the purchasing funnel. The subscription offer is introduced here but detailed below.

03Trust Bar

Five credibility signals positioned immediately below the hero before any scroll. In health DTC, trust is the primary currency — establishing it at this point intercepts buyer hesitation before product details are explored. The dark background creates a visual break that draws the eye down the page.

04Hero Product Feature

Full product presentation for Moisture Oyster Lubricant Eye Drops with the subscription toggle directly on the card. Showing subscribe vs one-time pricing at homepage level is the primary mechanism for increasing customer lifetime value. This block condenses into a product grid when the catalog grows to Phase 2.

05Eye Health Education

A problem agitation block placed deliberately after the product introduction — not before it. Users who have seen the product are primed to contextualise the statistics as personal relevance. The three US-specific stats (16M Americans, 7hr screen time, $4B market) shift the visitor from passive interest to active need, creating urgency before they reach the subscription pitch below.

06How It Works

A 3-step visual guide addressing the subscription model's friction points before they become objections. Transparency about the process — choose frequency, free delivery, cancel anytime — reduces anxiety and encourages users to opt for recurring delivery over one-time purchase. The block frames subscription as a service, not a commitment.

07About Us

Brand humanisation placed in the lower half of the page, after all product information has been absorbed. Consumers connect with authentic founder stories — this section builds emotional resonance that drives long-term retention rather than single-order conversion. A photo placeholder anchors the human element.

08Testimonials

Peer validation as the final conversion nudge before the email capture. Three cards with verified badges, geographic attribution, and use-case context (lens wearer, screen worker) provide relatable proof for the target audience. Positioned late in the scroll journey to catch high-intent users who are close to deciding.

09Coming Soon / Teaser

A dual-purpose block: builds anticipation for the eye vitamins launch and captures email leads for future product announcements. The email capture here is warmer than a generic newsletter signup — visitors who reach this point are already engaged with the brand. At Phase 2, this block is replaced by the full product grid.
